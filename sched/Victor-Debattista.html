The impact of spirals on disks: migration and vertical bending modes


Transient spirals are important drivers of various phenomena in galactic disks. In this talk I will present some of these effects. By far the most important is the radial migration of stars. I will present this phenomenon and describe evidence for it in the Milky Way and in external galaxies. I will also describe the effect of spirals on breathing motions within the disk; these motions have been seen in the Gaia data and I will discuss these results. Finally I will show that spirals are responsible for weak bending modes (at best).
