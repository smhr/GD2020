Secular evolution within galaxy disks


It has recently become clear that many aspects of disk galaxy structure today have been created by secular evolution, with spirals being one of the principal drivers. They rearrange the distribution of angular momentum and mass within the disk, mix stars and gas in a radially diffusive manner, contribute to increased random motion with stellar age, flatten rotation curves, etc. This talk will attempt to review all these aspects of spiral-driven evolution within a disk galaxy.
